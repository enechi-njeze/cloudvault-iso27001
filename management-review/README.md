## Purpose

This folder contains the Management Review records for the CloudVault FHX ISMS. ISO 27001 Clause 9.3 requires top management to review the ISMS at planned intervals to ensure its continuing suitability, adequacy, and effectiveness. The management review is not a technical meeting: it is a senior leadership meeting where the ISMS is evaluated from a business and risk perspective.

For CloudVault FHX, the management review is attended by the System Owner (Dr. Patricia Owens), the ISSO/ISSM (Enechi P.C. Njeze), the Privacy Officer (Sandra Voss), and the Authorizing Official's representative. Reviews are conducted semi-annually, with outputs informing the next risk assessment and ISMS improvement cycle.

## Required Management Review Inputs (Clause 9.3.2)

The following inputs must be addressed at every management review:

| Input | Source | CloudVault FHX Example |
|---|---|---|
| Status of previous review actions | Action log | Corrective actions from H2 2025 review |
| Changes to internal and external issues | Context review | New IRS Pub 1075 guidance, new agency onboarding |
| Information security performance feedback | Metrics dashboard | Vulnerability scan trends, incident count, POA&M aging |
| Nonconformities and corrective actions | Corrective actions register | Open corrective actions by severity |
| Monitoring and measurement results | ConMon reports | Monthly scan results, patching SLA compliance |
| Audit results | Internal audit reports | Q1 and Q2 internal audit findings |
| Fulfillment of information security objectives | Objectives tracker | ISMS objectives achieved vs. planned |
| Feedback from interested parties | Stakeholder register | Agency feedback, FedRAMP PMO status |
| Risk assessment results and treatment plan status | Risk register | High and Critical risks by status |
| Opportunities for continual improvement | ISMS improvement log | Process gaps identified in current cycle |

## Required Management Review Outputs (Clause 9.3.3)

Every management review must produce documented decisions and actions on: continual improvement opportunities for the ISMS, changes needed to ISMS requirements, resource needs, and any changes to the information security objectives. These outputs are tracked in the corrective actions register and reported at the next management review.

## Why Management Review Matters

ISO 27001 certification auditors look at management review records to confirm that senior leadership is genuinely engaged with the ISMS, not just the security team. A management review that addresses all required inputs with specific data, produces concrete decisions, and shows follow-through on previous decisions is strong evidence of a mature ISMS. A management review that is generic, lacks data, or shows no decisions fails to demonstrate top management commitment under Clause 5.1.
